ABOUT
our manifesto

SheRises is not a magazine. It's not a blog. It's a declaration — that women's stories are not secondary, their struggles are not niche, and their power is not optional. We exist to document, celebrate, and accelerate the rise of women worldwide.

How We Started

SheRises was born in a small apartment in Nairobi in 2018, from a conversation between three women journalists who were exhausted by the way women's stories were covered — when they were covered at all. Too often, women appeared in the news as victims, as accessories to men's narratives, or as inspirational exceptions who "made it" despite all odds.

We wanted something different. A platform where women were the protagonists of their own stories. Where complexity was welcomed. Where a woman farmer in Bangladesh and a CEO in Berlin could both find themselves reflected in our pages.

Seven years later, SheRises reaches 3.4 million readers monthly across 190 countries. We publish in six languages. We have broken stories that changed policy. We have introduced the world to women who deserved global platforms. And we are just getting started.

What We Believe

We believe that journalism can be feminist without losing its rigor. We believe that storytelling is one of the most powerful tools for social change. We believe that the most marginalized women — those at the intersection of multiple forms of discrimination — deserve the most amplification, not the least.

We believe that women's empowerment is not a "women's issue" — it is the central issue of our time, connected to everything from climate change to economic stability to democratic governance to global health. When women rise, humanity rises.

And we believe that the truth, told beautifully and without flinching, can change the world. That's why we write.
